Discussion

The fact is that risk is many things to many people.
For example:
1. Someone investing might be very interested in market volatility.
1. Someone borrowing would like to see the markets stable.
You can make the numbers say whatever you want them to say. For example, there is a 0.04% chance that a given stock will drop by 20%. This means that it is a very safe bet if you want to buy it. Now look at this:
The probability of the stock falling by 1% is 25%
The probability of the stock falling by 0.5% is 65%
etc.

As you can see, there is a very high probability that the stock price will change, just by how much is governed by different probabilities.

To find out by how much, you must follow the stock. Read the news, follow the company, etc.

There is a trick that says all numbers in the world contain the digit 3. This is a trick on percentages. Imagine infinity was number 9; then 10% of the numbers would have '3' in them. But infinity is not 9, so let's say it's 99.

3 + 13 + 23 + 30, 31, .. 39 + 43...

gives 19%. See as you increase infinity the number increases and tends to 100%.
